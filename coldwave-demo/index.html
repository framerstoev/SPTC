Interpretation

  • Cold-Wave Score higher = more resilient.
  • Hybrid score uses potential + observed performance.
  • Potential-only score uses Tier 1/2 only.
  • Evidence level shows observed support behind each score.
  • NPMRDS2 2025 volume is profile demand weight, not actual event-day volume.

How to read this panel

  • Composite score: higher = more resilient.
  • Hybrid score: 0.5 * potential resilience + 0.5 * observed resilience.
  • Potential-only: Tier 1/2 potential resilience only.
  • Evidence level: direct NPMRDS observed support status.
  • Observed curve: available only with direct NPMRDS support.
How to read Q(t)

Conceptual resilience pattern: performance may drop during an event and recover afterward. This schematic is for interpretation only; section-specific observed data are shown in the Q(t) chart.

Measured Q(t) curves may fluctuate because hourly speeds vary with congestion, sample size, incidents, and baseline-profile differences. The observed resilience score summarizes overall event/recovery behavior rather than requiring a perfectly smooth decline-and-recovery shape.

Observed normalized speed performance Q(t)
Q(t) = speed / baseline speed profile. Q = 1 means baseline-level speed performance.
Event and recovery observations are plotted from hourly NPMRDS data. Baseline is used as the normalization reference; baseline-period raw observations are not shown as plotted points in this prototype. Dashed lines indicate 80% and 90% baseline speed thresholds.
